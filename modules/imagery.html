Imagery LOCKED v2.1

All non-text visual content the AI fetches or proposes for a deck.

Plain-English summary. Whenever a deck needs a picture, logo, flag, or icon — not text — this module decides what to use, where to get it from, and whether the consultant can legally include it. One module covers cover photos, competitor logos for peer analysis, country flags for PESTEL slides, peer-map firm icons, and PESTEL center sector logos. The narrow "Cover Photo" name in the locked spec undersells the scope; the spec actually splits into 4 sub-modules covering all visual non-text content.

What it does

  • Picks an image when the consultant asks for one — cover photo, competitor logo, country flag, sector icon — using a 6-tier decision tree (user upload first, AI-gen last).
  • Tracks the license for every image — a separate ledger records source URL, license type, attribution, expiry, and whether the consultant can legally use it for this client engagement.
  • Refuses to fake real-firm visuals — server-side regex blocks AI-generated photos of real company buildings, employees, or HQs. AI-gen is allowed for abstract/illustrative imagery only.
  • Surfaces in the add-in — every image shape gets a license badge in the trust pane, just like text shapes get a traffic-light claim verdict.

How the picker works (6-tier decision tree)

TierSourceUse case
1User-uploadedConsultant has a specific image and uploads it directly. Highest priority.
2Firm IR press kitFor cover photos: scrape investor relations sites for officially-sanctioned firm imagery (logos, building shots, product photos).
3H&C curated library~$3k seed for 80-120 sector images. Pre-vetted, license-clean, brand-safe. Recommended default.
4Licensed stockShutterstock Unlimited (primary) + Getty editorial (fallback for news-style imagery).
5AI-genDALL-E 3 / Imagen — abstract/illustrative ONLY. Server-side regex blocks "brand+building", "brand+HQ", "brand+employees" prompts. Tenant opt-in.
6H&C placeholderGeneric graceful fallback when nothing else works.

The 4 sub-modules (locked spec)

DR-4a Cover Photo (~9-10d build)

The 6-tier picker for the title-slide cover image. Most engineering effort lives here. Per-tier license persistence; click-to-trace UX in the trust pane.

DR-4b Logo Swap (~3d build)

Replace consultant-uploaded firm-logo placeholders with real logos via Logo.dev (Clearbit successor) or licensed alternatives. Validates rights-of-use field; fail-closed if no license.

DR-4c Peer-Map Native Rebuild (~5d build)

For peer-analysis slides (e.g., "X vs competitors A/B/C"). Generates a native PowerPoint shape map with firm icons placed by industry segment. Not a pasted image.

DR-4d PESTEL Center Logo (~2d build)

For PESTEL slides (Political/Economic/Social/Technological/Environmental/Legal). The center logo + surrounding country flags + sector icons. Reuses Tier 3 H&C library + Tier 4 stock.

Image classification cascade (4-tier)

Behind the scenes, every image gets classified to decide what to do with it (replace? swap? keep?). The cascade goes cheapest-first:

  • Heuristics — file size, dimensions, aspect ratio, EXIF data. Free.
  • OCR — extract text from logos and brand marks. Cheap.
  • CLIP — semantic similarity matching for "is this a logo?" or "is this a building?". Cheap-ish.
  • Vision LLM — Gemini Flash for cost-sensitive, Sonnet 4.5 for hard cases. ~$150/year for 130K images at scale.

What's deliberately out of scope

Employee photos / people headshots. Privacy + GDPR + ethics concern. The system never auto-fetches photos of real people. Consultants can manually upload (e.g., from a client-provided org chart), but the AI will not propose them. Confirm with Filipe before relaxing.
