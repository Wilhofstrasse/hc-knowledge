Trust apparatus LOCKED v2.1 v2.1.1 amendment pending

The system that tracks what's true in your deck and who said it.

Plain-English summary. Every number, statement, image, and slide layout in a deck has provenance: where it came from, what level of trust it carries, who attested to it, and what's been changed since. The trust apparatus is the audit committee's answer to "show me you didn't make this up." Four invariants work together to make this possible — they look like separate systems, but architecturally they're one thing: the trust contract.

What it does

  • Tells the consultant the truth-status of every shape — green / yellow / orange / red badge per claim, before they hit "send."
  • Logs every action with tamper-proof retention — what changed, who changed it, when, why, against what source.
  • Enforces the send-gate — if a deck has unattested claims and goes to a client, the consultant gets blocked or sees a warning footer rendered into the slide itself.
  • Survives offline, copy-paste, and the deck leaving the H&C perimeter — disclaimer footers and ledger entries are baked into the .pptx file, not just the add-in.

How it works (4 components, plain-English)

1. Library — the vetted-content shelf

A 3-tier library (corporate / tenant / user) holds approved templates and slide patterns. Promotion path: a slide a consultant likes goes from project → user library → tenant library → corporate library, with partner sign-off at each step. Anything in the corporate library has been reviewed and is safe to use across all tenants. Lower tiers are progressively more flexible. "Where does this slide come from?" — always answerable.

2. Claim Ledger — current truth per shape

For every text shape and number on every slide, a row records: what the AI wrote, where it came from (source kind, citation pointer), what the confidence tier is (green = source-backed and high-confidence; yellow = source-backed but estimate; orange = consultant said so without partner co-sign; red = blocked, no source). This is the traffic-light per shape that the consultant sees in the add-in pane. Confidence is capped, not promised.

3. External Audit — tamper-proof action log

Every fill, edit, override, attestation, and send fires a row to a separate WORM (Write-Once-Read-Many) store at Cloudflare R2 with Object Lock COMPLIANCE retention — 7 years default, 10 years for banking and pharma sectors. Encrypted prompts; dual-key ceremony required to ever read them back (legal + infosec must both consent). This is the audit-committee-grade artifact. Even Filipe can't unilaterally delete an audit row.

4. Disclaimer Flag — the gap-honesty layer

Two layers: (a) hard gate inside the add-in when the consultant clicks Send/Export and unattested claims exist; (b) soft fallback rendered into the .pptx as a footer for paths Office.js can't intercept (Save As, AirDrop, email-attach, Teams share). The footer is removable from the deck itself — not enforcement, but a visible flag the consultant has to actively delete to misrepresent. Honest control, not a fortress.
