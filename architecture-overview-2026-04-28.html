1. Library Locked v2.0

Comment on §1. Library Locked v2.0
What it is

The shelf of pre-designed H&C slides you stamp + fill. Three shelves stacked together:

  • Corporate — official H&C templates, vetted by the design team
  • Office (Tenant) — your office's templates (H&C Switzerland, H&C Germany, etc. — separated)

  • When you leave H&C: Personal Library archives to H&C, you can request a personal copy with legal sign-off

2. Claim Ledger Locked v2.0

Comment on §2. Claim Ledger Locked v2.0
What it is

An invisible spreadsheet living inside every deck. One row per fact. Every row says: "the deck claims X; the source is Y; here's the quote." If a fact has no source, the row says "blocked" and that cell renders as [unverified] in the deck.

Four states a claim can be in
  • Source-backed — points to a real document (SEC filing, annual report). Confidence 85-99%.
  • Estimate-band — "between $5bn and $10bn" range. Marked clearly.
  • Consultant-attested — you said "I know this from client work last week." Free-text reason. Partner has to co-sign before client send.
  • Blocked — no source, no attestation. Shows as [unverified].
How it connects
  • Reads from Source Store to find supporting quotes
  • Writes to External Audit Log every time a claim is created or changed
  • Wrapped in Tenant Isolation (H&C-CH claims invisible to H&C-DE)
  • Feeds the Traffic Light colors and the Source Click-through panel
How it shows up in the add-in
  • Traffic light dot on every shape (green/yellow/orange/red)
  • Click any shape → side panel shows the source URL + exact quote
  • Full Cockpit shows every claim across the deck, filterable by tier
build: BE-A0 backend + MA-A traffic-light stub (~part of )

3. Asset Ledger v2.1 spec'd today

Comment on §3. Asset Ledger v2.1 spec'd today
What it is

Same idea as Claim Ledger but for images, not facts. One row per image used in the deck. Each row says: where the image came from, what license it has, who owns the rights, when the license expires. No image gets into the deck without a row.

Why it's separate from Claim Ledger

Claims are about truth (was the number right?). Images are about rights (do we have permission?). Different lifecycles, different rules. Codex flagged that mixing them would pollute both.

How it connects
  • Cover Photo Picker writes to it for every cover photo decision
  • Logo Swap and Peer-Map Rebuild (later modules) will also write here
  • Wrapped in Tenant Isolation
  • Feeds External Audit Log
How it shows up in the add-in
  • Mostly invisible — but if a license expires within 30 days, you get a notification in the Cockpit
build: DR-4a (Phase 2)

4. Source Store v2.1 spec'd today

Comment on §4. Source Store v2.1 spec'd today
What it is

A per-firm filing cabinet for every PDF, web page, and dataset the tool reads. The tool breaks each source into paragraphs and indexes them. When the AI writes a claim, it has to point to a specific paragraph in this cabinet.

What goes in
  • SEC EDGAR filings (free, US public firms — most reliable)
  • Annual reports / 10-Ks (PDF, parsed via docling)
  • Investor relations pages (HTML)
  • Companies House (UK) and GLEIF (legal entity identifiers)
  • PDFs you upload yourself
How it connects
  • Feeds Claim Ledger (every claim points to a paragraph here)
  • Feeds Grounding Engine (the LLM gets these paragraphs as context)
  • Wrapped in Tenant Isolation + EU Data Residency
How it shows up in the add-in
  • You upload PDFs via the JumpStart flow
  • The Cockpit shows which sources are stale (older than 14 days, or 404 now)
build: BE-A0

5. Grounding Engine v2.1 spec'd today

Comment on §5. Grounding Engine v2.1 spec'd today
What it is

The thing that forces every statement to cite a source. Built on Anthropic's native Citations API — when the AI writes, the citation pointers are produced server-side, not by the model. The model literally cannot make up sources anymore.

Two safety nets
  • Anthropic Citations — guarantees every quoted text is a real substring of the source document (server-parsed)
  • Pydantic validator + re-ask — if the model still slips a wrong quote, validation fails and it re-asks. After 3 failed retries, the claim gets blocked.
How it connects
  • Reads from Source Store (paragraphs as context)
  • Writes to Claim Ledger (every claim with its source pointer)
  • Writes to External Audit Log
How it shows up in the add-in
  • Traffic light per shape
  • Click shape → source panel slides open
  • Toggle: "highlight all claims by confidence" across the deck
  • Full Cockpit (separate larger view): filter, bulk-attest, audit export
build: BE-A0 backend + MA-A UI stub + MA-F full Cockpit

6. Cover Photo Picker v2.1 spec'd today

Comment on §6. Cover Photo Picker v2.1 spec'd today
What it is

Picks the right cover photo when you regenerate a deck. Six tiers, tried in order — first one that works wins:

  1. Your own upload (most defensible)
  2. The firm's official press kit (their own brand-controlled image)
  3. H&C internal sector library (80-120 hand-curated images, one-time $3k seed)
  4. Licensed stock (Shutterstock primary, Getty fallback)
  5. AI-generated abstract image ONLY if your office opted in. NEVER fake a real firm's HQ. Hard regex block at server.
  6. Plain placeholder if all else fails (visible signal, not a silent fallback)
Why this design

Most consultant decks today get sued for using unlicensed photos. This module makes that structurally impossible. Every image has rights tracking from minute one.

How it connects
  • Writes to Asset Ledger (license + provenance per image)
  • Writes to External Audit Log
  • Wrapped in Tenant Isolation + EU Data Residency
How it shows up in the add-in
  • Cover slide gets the chosen image with a small license badge in the audit pane
  • If tier 6 (placeholder) fires, slide is visibly tagged so you know to fix it
build: DR-4a Phase 2

7. Tenant Isolation Locked v2.0

Comment on §7. Tenant Isolation Locked v2.0
What it is

The wall between H&C-Switzerland, H&C-Germany, and any future external customer. Even if you're co-editing a deck and you accidentally email it to the wrong office, their tool can't read your claim ledger or audit trail. Encrypted with keys only your office holds.

How it connects
  • Wraps every other module — every Claim, Asset, Source, Audit row carries a tenant ID and is encrypted with that office's key
How it shows up in the add-in
  • Mostly invisible — but if you open someone else's deck from a different office, you'll see "Read-only — different tenant" instead of editable claims
foundational — every module reads/writes through this

8. External Audit Log Locked v2.0

Comment on §8. External Audit Log Locked v2.0
What it is

A tamper-proof cloud diary. Every fill, every image swap, every consultant attestation, every disclaimer click — gets duplicated to a Cloudflare R2 bucket with Object Lock COMPLIANCE retention. Even an admin can't delete a row. Default retention: 7 years (banking 10y, pharma 10y).

Why it matters

Compliance. If a partner-meeting goes sideways and a claim gets disputed, you can pull the exact source the AI saw at the time. Plus encrypted prompt storage means a leaked .pptx doesn't expose strategy text — a journalist couldn't reverse-engineer the audit.

How it shows up in the add-in
  • Cockpit has "Export Audit Report" → PDF + CSV for partner review
foundational — every module writes here

9. Disclaimer Flag Locked v2.0

Comment on §9. Disclaimer Flag Locked v2.0
What it is

The "AI was here, reviewed by a human" stamp. Two layers:

  • Inside the add-in — hard gate when you click "Send to client" (blocks until you've acknowledged AI use)
  • Inside the deck itself — small footer "AI-assisted, user-reviewed" rendered into the .pptx, so the recipient sees it no matter how the file arrived

Why two layers: Office.js can detect when you click the add-in's Send button, but it can NOT detect File→Save As, AirDrop, drag to email, etc. So we render the footer right into the deck — it travels with the file regardless.

How it shows up in the add-in
  • Banner at top of pane: "This deck was AI-assisted. You acknowledged review on DD.MM.YYYY"
build: MA-A (part of foundation)
